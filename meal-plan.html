Sunday Prep Sequence — 60 Minutes for 2 People

Everything runs in parallel. Do not cook sequentially — that's how it takes 3 hours. Start everything that has passive cook time first, then chop while it cooks.

0:00
Start pressure cooker: Spiced Lentil Dal with Cauliflower (Recipe #23) — double batch 25–30 min hands-off. This is Mon + Thu dinner for both. While it cooks, do everything else.
0:03
Hard-boil 12–14 eggs 12 min. Armaan 3/day × 5 days = 15 eggs. Akshita 2/day × 3 days = 6. Buy 2 dozen total.
0:05
Sheet pan: roast bell peppers + broccoli + zucchini 400°F, 22 min. These go in Black Bean Salad (Tue + Thu lunch), Paneer Bowl (Wed), and stir-fry base.
0:08
Chop the week: dice 3 onions, mince 1 garlic bulb, grate 2 tbsp ginger — store in small jars The single biggest weekday time-saver. Put in jars in fridge. Eliminates daily friction completely.
0:20
Make Asian Dense Bean Salad (Recipe #07) — double batch No-cook assembly. Will be Sun lunch + Tue lunch. Dress half now, leave half undressed for Tue.
0:30
Eggs done → cool in ice water, peel half now Store unpeeled eggs in carton in fridge (last longer). Peel only what you'll eat in 2 days.
0:32
Make Black Bean & Roasted Veg Salad (Recipe #27) base — no dressing yet Grab roasted veg from oven, combine with beans, spinach, seeds. Pack dressing separately. Mon + Thu lunch.
0:40
Dal done → portion into 4 large containers (labeled) 2 for Mon/Thu dinner, 2 in freezer as backup. Dal improves in flavor overnight. Add water when reheating.
0:45
Portion whey shake bags (Armaan's snacks) 5 pre-measured bags. Takes 5 min. Zero thinking on weekday afternoons.
0:50
Fridge organize + label everything Masking tape + sharpie on every container. Write name + date. Move old to front, new to back (FIFO).
1:00
Done. Wash everything immediately. Fill sink with hot soapy water now — not later. Pots with stuck dal: fill with water + soap, leave 20 min. Wipes clean.

Fridge Layout

If you can't see it, you won't eat it. Eye level = this week's food.

Top shelf
(eye level)
This week's prepped containers. Dal portions, bean salad (undressed), roasted veg. Everything labeled with name + date. If it's here, it gets eaten.
Middle shelf
Tofu submerged in water in a sealed container (changes water every 2 days → lasts 5 days). Paneer same method. Greek yogurt tubs. Hard-boiled eggs in their carton.
Bottom shelf
(coldest)
Raw ground turkey if using. Opened canned beans in a sealed container. Anything that needs maximum cold.
Crisper drawers
Separate wet from dry. Left: broccoli, cauliflower, peppers (wet veg). Right: spinach bags lined with paper towel top + bottom (dry greens). Never mix — ethylene gas from one wilts the other. Garlic jar + ginger jar live here too.
Door shelves
Condiments only. Miso, tahini, tamari, ACV, hot sauce, mustard. Never store eggs or dairy here — door is 4–6°F warmer than back of fridge.
Freezer
2 portions of dal (backup — Wednesday cliff insurance). Frozen spinach + edamame bags standing upright like books. Extra tofu — freezing tofu changes texture to chewy/meaty, ideal for stir-fry. Label everything.

Container System

Glass only
Buy glass, not plastic
Microwave-safe without leaching. Doesn't absorb turmeric or curry smell. Lasts years. Pyrex or IKEA glass with clip lids. ~$30 to kit out fully.
Quantity
14+ containers minimum
4× large (dal, batch dishes). 4× medium (salads, individual portions). 4× small (sauces, dressings, minced garlic). 2× leak-proof Sistema for work bags.
Work bags
Leak-proof only for commuting
Keep 2 dedicated work containers. Pack the night before — morning you will not make good decisions. Pack with sauce in a separate 2 oz deli cup. Add dressing at work.
Moisture trick
Paper towel inside chopped veg
Lay a dry paper towel on top of chopped broccoli/peppers before sealing. Absorbs condensation. Extends shelf life from 2 days to 4–5 days.

Reheating Rules

Dal / lentils
Add water, cover, 70% power
Add 2 tbsp water before microwaving. Cover with damp paper towel. 70% power for 2 min, stir once. Full power makes them dry and stiff.
Tofu / Paneer
Stovetop only — 2 min dry pan
Microwave makes them rubbery. 2 min in a dry hot pan re-crisps the outside completely. Worth the extra pan.
Roasted veg
Microwave or eat room temp
For crisp texture: air fryer 5 min at 400°F. For quick/lazy: microwave with damp paper towel cover is fine — they don't degrade badly.
Eggs
Eat cold or 20 sec max
Hard-boiled eggs are best cold or room temp. If you must warm: max 20 seconds, no shell, uncovered. Never microwave a shell-on egg.

Cheat Day Protocol

Saturday is fully off. Eat whatever. This isn't a flaw in the system — it's what makes the system sustainable. The psychological reset prevents binge-restrict cycles. The metabolic refeed (especially carbs) signals to your body it's not in a deficit, which supports hormone function particularly important for Akshita's PCOS.

The only rule: Sunday grocery run and meal prep still happen. If you do nothing else, batch the lentils before bed Saturday night. That single move makes Sunday prep 40% faster.

The 10 Things People Forget

01
The Wednesday Cliff
Sunday batch runs out by Wed/Thu. Fix: dal in freezer as insurance, Wednesday top-up run for spinach + tomatoes.
02
Onion + garlic not prepped = you won't cook
Sunday: dice 3 onions, mince a full garlic bulb, grate 2 tbsp ginger. Store in labeled jars. This one task eliminates 80% of weekday friction.
03
Sauce rotation prevents flavor fatigue
Same base food, different sauce = different meal. Keep: tahini, miso, hot sauce, ACV. Rotate across the week so you're not eating identical bowls daily.
04
Containers run out mid-week
You pack 2 work lunches, come home without them. Monday batch containers are dirty. Wash or run dishwasher every 2 days without fail.
05
Spinach dies before you use it
Buy 1 bag Sunday, 1 bag Wednesday. Or go 100% frozen spinach — identical nutrition, zero waste, no wilting.
06
Salad dressing packed wrong
Never dress salad the night before. Pack dressing in a 2 oz deli cup separately. Add at lunch. Prevents soggy spinach soup by noon.
07
Breakfast is a separate system
Most prep guides only cover lunch/dinner. Breakfast = prepped lentils (Sunday batch) + hard-boiled eggs + 5 min tadka. Assembly, not cooking.
08
Not portioning for two different sizes
Armaan: 1.3× portions. Akshita: 1×. Pre-portion into separate labeled containers on Sunday. Guessing eyeball at meal time leads to one person always short or overeating.
09
Turmeric stains everything within 30 min
Don't let turmeric-coated pots sit. Fill with water + soap immediately. Turmeric on plastic containers becomes permanent. Stick to glass.
10
Work snack drawer
Keep a bag of almonds + walnuts at your desk. Prevents vending machine decisions at 3pm entirely. The cheapest nutrition upgrade you'll ever make.
